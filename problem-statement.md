# Utilization MIS

## Objective

Persistent visibility into team capacity and workload across the firm.

## Noted Problems

- Understanding who's working on what currently requires a weekly in-person meeting. That's a bandwidth drain and doesn't scale.
- No ongoing, accessible view of how loaded people are. Leadership has no way to check utilization without scheduling a conversation.
- Utilization per person isn't straightforward: an Associate 3 can handle ~3 mandates while an Associate 1 can handle ~1. Raw mandate counts are misleading without calibration.

## Suggested Solution

Three layers:

1. **Automated bandwidth collection.** Replace the weekly meetings with an automated system where every VP and associate submits updates (weekly or biweekly) on what they're working on and how much time they're spending. Collection method should be as frictionless as possible. Cross-check VP and associate submissions, flag discrepancies, summarize for Founder's Office sign-off (currently Ajder, ajder@indigoedge.com).

2. **Month-by-month utilization view (Pai's ask).** For the current IY, show total utilization per person on a monthly basis. Calibrate against each person's actual capacity using the existing bandwidth scoring table, converted to mandate equivalent units. Display utilization as both a percentage of calibrated capacity and the absolute calibrated value (e.g., bandwidth score and mandate equivalents). Exact format TBD through brainstorming.

3. **Person drill-down view (Shiv's ask).** Click into any fellow to see their month-by-month breakdown:
   - % utilization (of calibrated capacity)
   - Actual absolute bandwidth score
   - Number of projects being worked on, broken down by type
   - What those projects are

   This layers on top of the utilization view.
